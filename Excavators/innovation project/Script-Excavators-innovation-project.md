**Silas:** One of our biggest innovations is where we put the motors. In most robots, the motors are in the arm, making it heavy and bulky. In the Flexi-Digger, the heavy motors and batteries stay outside the cave with the operator.

**Luke:** We use thin steel cablesâ€”like bicycle brake cablesâ€”running through the spine to pull and bend the arm. This keeps the arm lightweight and safe.

**Nelson:** But how do we dig without breaking the vase? We invented a concept called "Granular Fluidization." Instead of a metal claw, the tip of our robot uses a "soft-air" jet.

**Luke:** It blows a gentle stream of air to loosen the dirt, making it act like a liquid, and then a vacuum hose sucks the dirt away. It reveals the artifact without ever scratching it.
