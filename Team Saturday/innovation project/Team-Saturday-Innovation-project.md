## 1. Define the Problem: "Permanent Erasure"

Vandals don't want to sell the history; they (often accidentally or out of boredom) destroy it.

### The Problem

Archaeological sites (like cave paintings or ancient stone walls) are often in open areas. People might spray paint "I was here," carve names into stones, or knock down walls for fun.

### The "No Undo" Button

Unlike a modern building, you can't just use "paint thinner" on a 5,000-year-old cave painting. The chemicals in the cleaner would destroy the ancient art underneath.

### The Speed

Vandalism happens in seconds. A looter might spend hours digging; a vandal needs only 10 seconds to ruin a site with a can of spray paint.

### Real-World Examples

- Vandals put public access to Rainbow Canyon in southern Utah at risk
- Graffiti, spray paint vandalism discovered inside Arches National Park

---

## 2. Refine the Solution: The "Acoustic & Visual" Site Guardian

Since vandalism happens so fast, your system needs to be proactive (stopping it before it happens) rather than just reactive (filming it while it happens).

### New Smart Features for Team Saturday to Research:

#### Feature A: The "Hiss" Sensor (Acoustic AI)

Research microphones that can specifically recognize the sound of a spray paint can. There is software that can distinguish between a bird chirping and the "hiss" of an aerosol can or the "clink" of a hammer hitting stone.

#### Feature B: The "Virtual Fence"

Instead of waiting for someone to touch the wall, use a "Lidar" or "Laser Curtain." If a hand crosses an invisible line 12 inches away from a cave painting, the system triggers.

#### Feature C: Active Deterrents (The Scarecrow Effect)

- **Voice Warning:** A speaker says: "Warning: You are in a protected heritage zone. Your photo has been uploaded to the Ranger station." (Most vandals will run away if they think they are being watched).
- **Flash Lighting:** A bright strobe light can ruin their "art" and make it impossible for them to see what they are doing.

To impress the FLL judges, Ryker, Huey, and Hayden should look up these real-life examples:
